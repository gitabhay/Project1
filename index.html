Pichai Sundararajan

Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Alphabet Inc. & its Subsidiary Google.


An Indian-American Business Executive

Here's the timeline of Sundar Pichai


  • On June 10, 1972, Pichai Sundararajan born in Madras (now Chennai), Tamil Nadu, India.

  • Sundar Pichai's mother, Lakshmi, was a stenographer.

  • Pichai"s father, Regunatha Pichai, was an electrical engineer at GEC, the British conglomerate.

  • Pichai completed schooling in Jawahar Vidyalaya Senior Secondary School in Ashok Nagar, Chennai and completed the Class XII from Vana Vani school at IIT Madras.

  • He earned his degree from IIT Kharagpur in metallurgical engineering and is a distinguished alumnus from that institution.

  • He holds an M.S. from Stanford University in materials science and engineering.

  • Pichai holds MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, where he was named a Siebel Scholar and a Palmer Scholar.

  • Pichai worked in engineering and product management at Applied Materials and in management consulting at McKinsey & Company.

  • In 2004, Pichai joined Google, where he led the product management.

  • He also led the innovation efforts for a suite of Google's client software products, including Google Chrome & Chrome OS, as well as being largely responsible for Google Drive.

  • On November 19, 2009, Pichai gave a demonstration of Chrome OS.

  • In 2011, the Chromebook was released for trial and testing.

  • On May 20, 2010, he announced the open-sourcing of the new video codec VP8 by Google and introduced the new video format, WebM.

  • On March 13, 2013, Pichai added Android to the list of Google products that he oversaw.

  • on August 10, 2015, Sundar Pichai was selected to become the next CEO of Google.

  • On October 24, 2015, he stepped into the new position at the completion of the formation of Alphabet Inc.

  • In 2014, Pichai had been suggested as a contender for Microsoft's CEO, a position that was eventually given to Satya Nadella.

  • In December 2017, Pichai was a speaker at the World Internet Conference in China.

  • In December 2019, Pichai became the CEO of Alphabet Inc.

  • In December 2020, Pichai delivered a speech virtually at the Singapore FinTech Festival emphasizing inclusive digital economy.

  • In 2022, Pichai received the Padma Bhushan from the Government of India, its third-highest civilian award.
